# 🔐 1.1: Security Control Categories & Types

---

## 1️⃣ What are Security Controls?

### 🧾 Definition:

Security controls are **measures put in place to protect systems, data, people, and facilities** 

---

### 🎯 Purpose:

* Prevent attacks
* Detect attacks
* Respond to incidents
* Reduce damage

---

## 2️⃣ Security Control Categories (VERY IMPORTANT)

👉 These describe **HOW the control is implemented**

---

### 💻 Technical Controls

* Implemented using:

  * Software
  * Hardware

#### 📌 Examples:

* Firewalls
* Antivirus
* OS policies

---

### 🧾 Managerial (Administrative) Controls

* Policies and procedures
* High-level guidance

#### 📌 Examples:

* Security policies
* Onboarding procedures
* Compliance rules

---

### 👥 Operational Controls

* Performed by **people**

#### 📌 Examples:

* Security guards
* Training programs
* Awareness campaigns

---

### 🏢 Physical Controls

* Protect **physical access**

#### 📌 Examples:

* Locks
* Fences
* Badge readers

---

## 🔥 KEY EXAM IDEA

👉 Same control type can exist in **different categories**

Example:

* Guard → Operational
* Lock → Physical
* Firewall → Technical

---

## 3️⃣ Control Types (WHAT the control DOES)

---

## 🛑 Preventive Controls

### 🎯 Purpose:

* **Stop an attack before it happens**

---

### 📌 Examples:

* Firewall rules
* Guard checking ID
* Door locks

---

## ⚠️ Deterrent Controls

### 🎯 Purpose:

* **Discourage attackers**

👉 Does NOT stop them, but makes them think twice

---

### 📌 Examples:

* Warning signs
* Splash screens
* Threat of punishment

---

## 🔍 Detective Controls

### 🎯 Purpose:

* **Detect that something happened**

---

### 📌 Examples:

* Logs
* Monitoring
* Motion sensors
* Patrol guards

---

## 🔧 Corrective Controls

### 🎯 Purpose:

* **Fix or recover AFTER an attack**

---

### 📌 Examples:

* Restore from backup
* Fire extinguisher
* Incident response

---

## 🔄 Compensating Controls

### 🎯 Purpose:

* **Alternative solution when primary control is not available**

---

### 📌 Examples:

* Firewall rule instead of patch
* Generator during power outage
* Separation of duties

---

## 📢 Directive Controls

### 🎯 Purpose:

* **Tell users what they SHOULD do**

---

### 📌 Examples:

* Policies
* Training
* “Authorized personnel only” sign

---

## ⚠️ IMPORTANT:

👉 Directive = weakest control
(because it depends on people following instructions)

---

## 4️⃣ Category + Type COMBINED (THIS IS TESTED)

👉 Every control has:

* A **category** (how)
* A **type** (what it does)

---

### 🔥 Examples:

| Control         | Category    | Type                   |
| --------------- | ----------- | ---------------------- |
| Firewall        | Technical   | Preventive             |
| Security policy | Managerial  | Directive              |
| Guard           | Operational | Preventive / Detective |
| Lock            | Physical    | Preventive             |
| Logs            | Technical   | Detective              |
| Backup restore  | Technical   | Corrective             |
| Warning sign    | Physical    | Deterrent              |

---

## 🧠 Memory Anchors (from video concepts)

* Preventive = stop
* Detective = find
* Corrective = fix
* Deterrent = scare
* Compensating = workaround
* Directive = tell

# 🧾 Flashcards – ANSWERS

---

### 1. What are the four categories of security controls?

**Technical, Managerial (Administrative), Operational, Physical**

---

### 2. What is a technical control?

A control implemented using **technology (hardware or software)**

---

### 3. What is a managerial control?

A control based on **policies, procedures, and governance**

---

### 4. What is an operational control?

A control performed by **people and processes**

---

### 5. What is a physical control?

A control that restricts **physical access to systems or locations**

---

### 6. What is a preventive control?

A control that **stops an attack before it happens**

---

### 7. What is a deterrent control?

A control that **discourages attackers but does not stop them**

---

### 8. What is a detective control?

A control that **detects and identifies an attack or event**

---

### 9. What is a corrective control?

A control that **fixes or restores systems after an incident**

---

### 10. What is a compensating control?

An **alternative control used when the primary control is not available**

---

### 11. What is a directive control?

A control that **instructs users on what they should do**

---

# ➕ Extra Recall – ANSWERS

---

### 12. What is the difference between control category and control type?

* **Category** = how the control is implemented
* **Type** = what the control does

---

### 13. Why can a single control fit into multiple categories?

Because controls can be applied in **different ways depending on implementation**

---

### 14. Why are directive controls considered weak?

Because they **depend on users following instructions**

---

### 15. When would you use a compensating control?

When the **primary control cannot be implemented**, often temporarily

---
