# 🔐 1.2 : Physical Security 

---

## 1️⃣ Why Physical Security Matters

**Key Idea:**
Security is not just digital — **physical access = full compromise**.

👉 If an attacker gets physical access:

* They can steal devices
* Install malware
* Bypass logical security controls

---

## 2️⃣ Physical Barriers (First Line of Defense)

### 🚧 Barricades / Bollards

* Prevent vehicles from entering restricted areas
* Often **concrete or metal posts**
* Also act as **visual deterrents**

**Purpose:**

* Channel people through controlled entry points
* Stop cars, trucks, or forced entry

---

### 🌉 Natural Barriers

* Water, bridges, terrain
* Force access through **controlled paths**

---

## 3️⃣ Controlled Entry Systems

### 🚪 Access Control Vestibule (Mantrap)

**Definition:**
A secured room where you must pass through **one door at a time**.

**How it works:**

* Only **one door opens at a time**
* May require:

  * Badge
  * Biometrics
  * Security verification

**Purpose:**

* Prevent tailgating
* Ensure only authorized individuals enter

---

## 4️⃣ Fencing

### 🛑 Types:

* **Transparent fence** → visible through
* **Opaque fence** → blocks visibility

### 🔒 Security Features:

* Tall height
* Strong materials
* Razor wire (high security)

**Goal:**
Prevent or slow unauthorized access

---

## 5️⃣ Surveillance Systems

### 📹 CCTV (Closed-Circuit Television)

**Features:**

* Multiple cameras networked together
* Centralized recording system
* 24/7 monitoring

### Advanced Capabilities:

* Motion detection
* Facial recognition
* License plate recognition

**Purpose:**

* Monitoring
* Evidence collection
* Deterrence

---

## 6️⃣ Security Guards

### 👮 Roles:

* Verify identity of individuals
* Monitor entry points
* Respond to incidents

### Two-Person Integrity

* Two guards present
* Prevents one person from bypassing controls

---

## 7️⃣ Identification Badges

### 🪪 Features:

* Photo
* Name
* Organization details

### Functions:

* Visual identification
* Access control (used with badge readers)
* Activity logging (entry/exit tracking)

---

## 8️⃣ Lighting (Simple but Powerful)

### 💡 Why it matters:

* Removes hiding spots
* Increases visibility for cameras

### Best Practices:

* Cover all areas
* Proper angles (important for facial recognition)
* Especially important in:

  * Parking lots
  * Entrances

---

## 9️⃣ Motion Detection Technologies

### 🔥 Infrared (IR)

* Detects heat / movement
* Works in dark environments
* Used in:

  * Cameras
  * Motion sensors

---

### 📡 Microwave Sensors

* Covers **large areas**
* Detects movement using radio waves

---

### 🔊 Ultrasonic Sensors

* Uses sound waves
* Detects movement via reflections
* Can also detect collisions

---

### 👣 Pressure Sensors

* Detect weight/force changes
* Example: floor sensors

---

## 🔟 Summary of Controls (Big Picture)

| Control Type | Example                    | Purpose              |
| ------------ | -------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Deterrent    | Lighting, cameras          | Discourage attackers |
| Preventive   | Fences, bollards, mantraps | Stop access          |
| Detective    | CCTV, motion sensors       | Detect activity      |
| Corrective   | Security guards            | Respond to incidents |

---

## 🧠 Memory Anchors

* 🚧 **Bollards = “Stop cars”**
* 🚪 **Mantrap = one person at a time**
* 📹 **CCTV = always watching**
* 👮 **Guards = human firewall**
* 💡 **Light = no hiding**
* 🌡️ **Infrared = heat detection**

---

## ✅ Final Insight

Physical security works in **layers**:

1. **Deter** (lights, cameras)
2. **Prevent** (fences, mantraps)
3. **Detect** (sensors, CCTV)
4. **Respond** (guards)

👉 If one layer fails, another still protects the system.
