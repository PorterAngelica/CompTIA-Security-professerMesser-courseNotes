# 🔐 1.2 : Change Management (Change Control)

---

## 1️⃣ What is Change Management?

**Definition:**
A **formal process** used to manage changes to systems (software, hardware, configurations) in a controlled and secure way.

👉 In large organizations:

* One change can affect **hundreds or thousands of systems** 

---

## 2️⃣ Why Change Management is Critical

Without control:

* Systems break
* Inconsistencies occur
* Security vulnerabilities increase

With control:

* Maintain **availability (uptime)**
* Ensure **consistency**
* Reduce **risk of errors**

---

## 3️⃣ Key Goals of Change Control

* ✅ Prevent system failures
* ✅ Ensure everyone is informed
* ✅ Maintain stability
* ✅ Reduce human error
* ✅ Improve security posture

---

## 4️⃣ Change Control Process (Step-by-Step)

### 📝 1. Request / Documentation

* Submit a **change request form**
* Include:

  * Reason for change
  * Scope (what systems are affected)
  * Impact

---

### 📊 2. Risk Analysis

* Evaluate:

  * Risk of implementing change
  * Risk of NOT implementing change

---

### 👥 3. Change Control Board (CCB)

* Reviews request
* Approves or rejects change

---

### 📅 4. Scheduling

* Choose **safe time** (off-hours, weekends)
* Avoid peak business times

---

### ⚙️ 5. Implementation

* Apply change to systems

---

### 🧪 6. Testing / Validation

* Users verify everything works correctly

---

## 5️⃣ Roles in Change Management

### 👤 Owner

* Requests change
* Verifies system after change

### 👥 Stakeholders

* People/departments affected by change
* Provide input

### 🧠 IT Team

* Implements technical changes

### 🏛️ Change Control Board (CCB)

* Makes final decision

---

## 6️⃣ Stakeholders (IMPORTANT for Exam)

👉 Stakeholders are often **hidden in questions**

Example:

* Change to shipping software
* Affects:

  * Shipping department
  * Accounting
  * Customers
  * Revenue

**Key idea:**
Even small changes can have **huge business impact**

---

## 7️⃣ Risk Considerations

### Risks of MAKING the change:

* Break systems
* Cause downtime
* Data corruption
* OS failure

### Risks of NOT making the change:

* Security vulnerabilities
* Exploits
* System instability

👉 Always balance **both sides**

---

## 8️⃣ Sandbox / Test Environment

### 🧪 Definition:

A **safe testing environment** separate from production

### Purpose:

* Test changes safely
* Simulate real systems
* Avoid impacting users

👉 Think:

* “Practice before going live”

---

## 9️⃣ Backout Plan (CRITICAL)

### 🔄 Definition:

A plan to **revert changes if something goes wrong**

### Examples:

* Uninstall patch
* Restore previous config
* Roll back update

👉 ALWAYS have a rollback plan

---

## 🔟 Backups

### 💾 Rule:

Before any change → **FULL BACKUP**

👉 Why:

* If rollback fails → restore backup

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ Timing of Changes

### Best Practice:

* Perform during:

  * Nights
  * Weekends
  * Maintenance windows

### Avoid:

* Peak business hours
* Critical business periods

### Example:

* Retail companies freeze changes during holidays

---

## 1️⃣2️⃣ Key Takeaways

* Change management = **controlled, approved changes**
* Always:

  * Document
  * Analyze risk
  * Get approval
  * Test
  * Have rollback plan
* Small changes can have **big impact**
* Security depends on **controlled updates**

---

## 🧠 Memory Anchors

* 📝 **“No form = No change”**
* 🧪 **Sandbox = practice arena**
* 🔄 **Backout plan = undo button**
* 💾 **Backup = last safety net**
* 👥 **Stakeholders = everyone affected**

## 🧾 Flashcards (Active Recall)

1. **Q:** What is change management?
   **A:** A formal process to control system changes.

2. **Q:** Who approves changes?
   **A:** Change Control Board (CCB).

3. **Q:** What is a sandbox?
   **A:** A test environment separate from production.

4. **Q:** What is a backout plan?
   **A:** A method to reverse a change if it fails.

5. **Q:** Why are stakeholders important?
   **A:** They are impacted by changes and must be considered.

6. **Q:** When should changes be implemented?
   **A:** During off-hours or maintenance windows.

7. **Q:** What must you do before any change?
   **A:** Create a backup.

---

## ✅ Final Insight

👉 **Good security = controlled change**

Most real-world outages happen because:

* Someone made a change
* Without testing
* Without rollback
