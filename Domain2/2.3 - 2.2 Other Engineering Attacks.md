# 🔐 2.2: Misinformation, Disinformation & Influence Attacks

---

## 1️⃣ What are Misinformation & Disinformation?

---

### 🧾 Definition:

Both are forms of **false information** used in social engineering

---

## 🧠 Misinformation

### 🧾 Definition:

False or incorrect information that is **shared without intent to harm**

---

## 🎯 Disinformation

### 🧾 Definition:

False information that is **intentionally created to deceive or manipulate**

---

### ⚠️ Key Idea:

👉 Both are **factually incorrect**
👉 Disinformation = intentional

---

---

# 2️⃣ Purpose of These Attacks

---

👉 Designed to:

* Confuse people
* Divide groups
* Influence opinions

---

### ⚠️ Key Idea:

👉 This is psychological manipulation

---

---

# 3️⃣ Influence Campaigns

---

## 🧾 Definition:

Coordinated efforts to spread misinformation/disinformation to influence public opinion

---

### 🎯 Where they happen:

* Social media
* Advertising platforms

---

### 🌍 Who uses them:

* Nation-states
* Organized groups

---

---

# 4️⃣ How Influence Campaigns Work

---

## 🧠 Step-by-Step Process

---

### 1. Create Fake Accounts

* Multiple fake users
* Controlled by attacker

---

### 2. Post False Content

* Fake article
* Misleading information

---

### 3. Amplify Content

* Like
* Share
* Follow

---

### 4. Algorithm Boost

* Social media shows popular posts
* More people see it

---

### 5. Real Users Spread It

* People believe it
* Share with others

---

### 6. Mass Media Picks It Up

* Becomes “news”
* Even though it started false

---

---

# 5️⃣ Why This Works

---

👉 Social media algorithms:

* Promote popular content
* Don’t verify truth

---

👉 People:

* Trust what they see often
* Trust what others share

---

---

# 6️⃣ Brand Impersonation Attacks

---

## 🧾 Definition:

Attackers use **well-known brand names** to gain trust

---

### 🎯 Examples:

* Coca-Cola
* McDonald’s

---

---

## 🧠 How it works:

1. Create fake websites using brand name
2. Get indexed by search engines
3. User searches brand
4. User clicks fake site

---

---

### ⚠️ Result:

* Fake offers
* Malware downloads
* Data theft

---

---

# 7️⃣ Malware from Fake Sites

---

## 🎯 What happens after infection:

* Ads displayed
* Activity tracking
* Data theft (**exfiltration**)

---

## 🧾 Exfiltration Definition:

Unauthorized transfer of data out of a system

---

---

# 8️⃣ Key Terms (NO CONFUSION)

---

## 🧾 Misinformation

False info (not intentional)

---

## 🧾 Disinformation

False info (intentional)

---

## 🧾 Influence Campaign

Coordinated manipulation effort

---

## 🧾 Exfiltration

Data stolen from system

---
