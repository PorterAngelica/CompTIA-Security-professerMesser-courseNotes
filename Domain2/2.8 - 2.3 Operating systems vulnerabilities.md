# 🔐 2.3: Operating system vulnerabilities

---

## 1️⃣ Why Operating System Patching Matters

---

### 🧾 Definition:

A **patch** is a software update that fixes:

* Security vulnerabilities
* Bugs
* System issues

---

### 🎯 Core Idea:

👉 Operating systems are the **foundation** of all computing

---

### ⚠️ Key Insight:

👉 If the OS is vulnerable → everything is vulnerable

---

---

# 🧠 Why OS Is a Target

---

👉 Attackers love operating systems because:

* Everyone uses them
* They control everything
* They are extremely complex

---

### ⚠️ Important Fact:

👉 Modern OS = **millions of lines of code**

---

👉 More code = more vulnerabilities

---

---

# 🧠 Hidden Reality (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

👉 Your OS ALREADY has vulnerabilities

👉 You just don’t know them yet

---

### 🧠 Process:

1. Vulnerability discovered
2. Reported to vendor
3. Patch created
4. Users install patch

---

---

# 🧠 🔥 ANALOGY (IMPORTANT)

---

## 🏠 House with Hidden Weak Spots

Imagine:

* Your house looks secure
* But there are **hidden cracks in the walls**

---

### 🧨 What happens:

* You don’t know where they are
* A burglar eventually finds one

---

### 🛠️ Patch = fixing the crack

---

### 🎯 Meaning:

👉 OS vulnerabilities already exist
👉 Patches fix them AFTER discovery

---

---

# 2️⃣ Patch Tuesday (Microsoft)

---

### 🧾 Definition:

**Patch Tuesday** = second Tuesday of every month when Microsoft releases updates

---

### 🎯 Purpose:

👉 Release multiple patches at once

---

### ⚠️ Key Idea:

👉 Many vulnerabilities are fixed at once

---

---

# 3️⃣ Types of Vulnerabilities (IMPORTANT TERMS)

---

## 🔺 Elevation of Privilege (EoP)

👉 Gaining higher permissions

---

## 🔓 Remote Code Execution (RCE)

👉 Running code remotely on a system

---

## 🚫 Security Feature Bypass

👉 Skipping security controls

---

---

# 4️⃣ Why You Must Patch Quickly

---

### ⚠️ Key Idea:

👉 Once a vulnerability is announced:

* Attackers analyze it
* Build exploits

---

### 🎯 Goal:

👉 Patch BEFORE attackers weaponize it

---

---

# 🧠 Real Timeline

1. Patch released
2. Vulnerability becomes public
3. Attackers reverse engineer
4. Exploit created

---

👉 If you patch early → SAFE
👉 If not → vulnerable

---

---

# 5️⃣ Patch Management (Best Practices)

---

## 🔹 1. Always Plan Updates

👉 Expect regular updates

---

## 🔹 2. Patch Quickly

👉 Reduce exposure time

---

---

## 🔹 3. Test Before Deployment (ENTERPRISE)

---

👉 In large environments:

* Test patch first
* Avoid breaking systems

---

---

## 🔹 4. Backup Before Updating

---

👉 If something fails → restore system

---

---

## 🔹 5. Expect Reboots

---

👉 Some patches:

* Modify core OS
* Require restart

---

---

# 6️⃣ Risk of Patching

---

👉 Patches can:

* Break applications
* Cause instability

---

### ⚠️ Key Idea:

👉 That’s why testing + backups are critical

---

---
