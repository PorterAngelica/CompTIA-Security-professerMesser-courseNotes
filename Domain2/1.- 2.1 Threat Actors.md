# 🔐 2.1: Threat Actors

---

## 1️⃣ What is a Threat Actor?

### 🧾 Definition:

A **threat actor** is an entity responsible for actions that negatively impact security 

👉 Also called:

* Malicious actor

---

### 🎯 Purpose of Understanding Threat Actors:

* Identify **who is attacking**
* Understand **why (motivation)**
* Predict **how they attack**

---

## 2️⃣ Key Characteristics of Threat Actors

👉 When analyzing attackers, look at:

---

### 💰 Resources

* Low → limited tools
* High → advanced attacks

---

### 🧠 Sophistication

* Low → script users
* High → build their own tools

---

### 🎯 Motivation

* Data theft
* Disruption
* Espionage
* Political reasons
* Financial gain

---

### 🌍 Location

* Internal (inside organization)
* External (outside attackers)

---

## 🔥 KEY EXAM IDEA

👉 Questions will often ask:

* WHO is the attacker?
* WHAT is their motivation?
* HOW advanced are they?

# 🧾 ANSWERS – Threat Actors

---

## 🔹 Flashcards

1. A threat actor is an entity that causes security harm (malicious actor).

2. Resources, sophistication, motivation, and location (internal/external).

3. A government-backed attacker with high resources and advanced capabilities.

4. **APT (Advanced Persistent Threat)** = long-term, highly sophisticated attack (often nation-state).

5. An attacker who uses tools/scripts without understanding them.

6. Political or ideological reasons.

7. They already have authorized access and know internal systems.

8. Financial gain (money).

9. Employees bypassing IT policies and creating their own systems/tools.
